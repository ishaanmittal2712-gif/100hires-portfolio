## Post 1 — Hybrid Engine Optimization (HEO)
**Source:** https://www.ewrdigital.com/blog/most-helpful-seo-aeo-influencers-to-follow
**Source 2:** https://cairrot.com/blog/best-ai-seo-aeo-speakers-influencers/

Jori Ford developed the concept of Hybrid Engine Optimization (HEO) 
— one of the most original frameworks to emerge from the AI SEO 
space in 2025.

Core argument: Traditional SEO optimizes for rankings. GEO optimizes 
for AI citations. HEO argues you need both simultaneously — and that 
the real competitive advantage lies in optimizing for AI agent 
interactions, not just AI search results.

The distinction matters: AI search results (like AI Overviews) are 
passive — they answer a query. AI agents are active — they research, 
compare, and make recommendations on behalf of users. Jori argues 
most SEOs are preparing for the wrong battle.

Her background: Software engineering + marketing — giving her a 
unique ability to bridge the gap between development and SEO in 
ways most practitioners cannot.

Key contribution at SEO Week 2025: Her insights on tracking crawls 
from AI and LLM bots inspired another practitioner to build a free 
WordPress plugin that allows sites to track LLM visibility without 
manually creating reports from server log files.

**Annotation:** Jori is the most technically original thinker on 
this list. HEO is not a repackaging of existing ideas — it's a 
genuinely new framework for thinking about AI visibility. Her 
software engineering background means her recommendations are 
implementable, not just theoretical. For a product-led B2B SaaS 
like 100Hires, understanding how AI agents evaluate and recommend 
ATS tools is directly relevant.

## Post 2 — AI Crawler Behavior Research
**Source:** https://www.ewrdigital.com/blog/most-helpful-seo-aeo-influencers-to-follow
**Source 2:** https://cairrot.com/blog/best-ai-seo-aeo-speakers-influencers/

Jori conducts empirical experiments to understand how AI crawlers 
actually interact with websites — not how they're supposed to, 
but how they actually do.

Key findings from her research:
- Most LLMs cannot render JavaScript during retrieval — meaning 
  JS-heavy pages are effectively invisible to AI crawlers
- Core performance metrics (CWV and TTFB) still matter for 
  real-time RAG retrieval
- AI crawlers behave fundamentally differently from Googlebot — 
  and most sites are not instrumented to even detect or measure 
  this behavior
- Server log analysis is the most reliable way to understand 
  actual AI crawler activity on your site

Talk at Tech SEO Connect 2025: "Beyond Googlebot: Evidence-Based 
Retrieval Experiments with AI Crawlers"

**Annotation:** While most AI SEO advice is based on correlation 
studies and educated guesses, Jori's work is based on controlled 
experiments with actual AI crawler behavior. This empirical approach 
is rare and makes her insights significantly more reliable than 
most published frameworks.
