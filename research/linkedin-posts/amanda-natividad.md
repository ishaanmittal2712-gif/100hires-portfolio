## Post 1 — Zero Click Marketing Framework
**Source:** https://meetsona.ai/blog/linkedin-top-ai-content-voices/
**Source 2:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/amandanat/

Amanda co-created the Zero Click Marketing framework with Rand 
Fishkin in 2022 — and it has become one of the most referenced 
content strategy concepts in the AI search era.

Core argument: Most content marketing is built around the assumption 
that people will click through to your site. In an AI-first world, 
that assumption is broken. AI systems answer questions directly, 
keeping users inside the AI interface rather than sending them 
to your site.

Zero Click Marketing reframes the goal: instead of optimizing 
for clicks, optimize for brand impressions, citations, and 
top-of-mind awareness — even when no click happens.

Key concepts:
- Algorithmic Capital: The accumulated trust and visibility a 
  brand builds across platforms that influences how algorithms 
  (including AI systems) perceive and recommend it
- Zero Click Content: Content designed to deliver value 
  entirely within the feed or AI response — building brand 
  affinity without requiring a click
- Audience research over keyword research: Understanding where 
  your audience actually spends time, rather than just what 
  they search for

Background that makes her credible: Former journalist, 
Le Cordon Bleu-trained chef, Fitbit B2B content lead, 
VP Marketing at SparkToro. Guest lecturer at Columbia Business 
School, Cornell, and Stanford.

**Annotation:** Amanda's Zero Click framework is the most 
practically useful mental model for content teams navigating 
the AI search transition. For 100Hires — a B2B SaaS competing 
for visibility in a space where buyers increasingly ask AI 
"what's the best ATS for small businesses?" — building 
Algorithmic Capital is the strategic imperative, not 
driving clicks to blog posts.

## Post 2 — Content Strategy for the AI Era
**Source:** https://meetsona.ai/blog/linkedin-top-ai-content-voices/

Amanda's consistent thesis across her 2025-26 content:

AI has made it easier than ever to produce content. Which means 
the bar for content that actually resonates with real humans 
has never been higher.

Her recommendations for content teams:
- Stop producing content for algorithms and start producing 
  content for specific humans in specific contexts
- Audience research is now more valuable than keyword research 
  — understanding where your audience spends attention is more 
  important than what they type into Google
- The brands that win in AI search are the ones that are 
  genuinely present and useful in the communities and 
  platforms where their audience already is

Platforms she actively uses to practice what she teaches: 
LinkedIn, SparkToro's own research tools, podcast appearances, 
and her Zero Click Marketing newsletter.

**Annotation:** Amanda represents the "human-first" school of 
AI content strategy. In a sea of technical GEO frameworks and 
AI optimization tactics, her perspective is the necessary 
counterbalance — a reminder that the end goal is resonance 
with real people, not citation by machines.
