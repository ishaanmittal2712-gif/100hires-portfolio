## Post 1 — The ROI of Generative Engine Optimization (July 2025)
**Source:** https://rosssimmonds.com/blog/roi-generative-engine-optimization/

Key argument: The question content teams are asking has shifted from 
"Are we producing enough content?" to "Is our content actually 
influencing LLMs and showing up where buyers get answers?"

Ross introduces RoGEO (Return on Generative Engine Optimization) as 
the new standard of measurement for performance content teams.

Core thesis: "Generative Engine Optimization isn't just a buzzword. 
It's a shift in strategy that goes beyond SEO. It's how smart brands 
are recalibrating their marketing engines for a world where clicks are 
vanishing, impressions are fragmented, and buyers are being served 
answers by AI before they ever hit your homepage."

Data point: His work shows brands can boost their citation rate in 
AI-generated answers by up to 40% through smart, data-led content 
distribution.

**Annotation:** Ross is the practitioner who made GEO measurable. 
For a B2B SaaS ATS like 100Hires competing for visibility in an 
AI-first search environment, his RoGEO framework is directly 
applicable — especially the distribution-first philosophy.

## Post 2 — Create Once, Distribute Forever
**Source:** https://firstpagesage.com/seo-blog/top-geo-consultants

Core philosophy: The breadth of a brand's content distribution 
directly determines whether AI systems encounter and cite that 
content in the first place.

Key insight: Ross argues that most brands underinvest in distribution 
and overinvest in production. In an AI-first world, a piece of content 
that exists in one place has far lower odds of being cited by LLMs than 
the same content distributed across YouTube, LinkedIn, Reddit, 
newsletters, and third-party publications.

Clients where this has been validated: Canva, Jobber, Procore.

**Annotation:** "Create Once, Distribute Forever" is the single most 
cited content philosophy in GEO discussions in 2025-26. It directly 
challenges the typical B2B SaaS content playbook of publish-and-hope, 
and reframes distribution as the primary growth lever.
