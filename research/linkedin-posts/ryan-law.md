## Post 1 — AI Search Research (Feb 2026)

In the last 3 months at Ahrefs, we analyzed over 1 billion data points 
across 11 studies. Here's what we learned about AI search optimization:

1. YouTube mentions are the single strongest predictor of AI visibility 
(correlation: 0.737) — stronger than Domain Rating, backlinks, or any 
traditional SEO factor.
2. For a given query, AI Mode and AI Overviews reach the same conclusions 
86% of the time — but cite almost entirely different sources (only 13.7% 
citation overlap).
3. Content length has essentially zero correlation with AI citations (0.04). 
53% of all AI Overview citations go to pages under 1,000 words.
4. Google still sends 345x more traffic than ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity 
combined — but ChatGPT accounts for 80%+ of all AI-driven website traffic.

**Why this matters:** Everyone's chasing the AI search dragon. Meanwhile, 
Google is still doing the heavy lifting. The fundamentals haven't changed. 
Make good stuff. Keep it current. Be where your audience is. AI search 
optimization isn't a revolution. It's another channel. Treat it like one.

**Annotation:** This post is backed by Ahrefs' proprietary 1B+ data point 
study — one of the largest AI search research projects published publicly. 
The YouTube correlation finding (0.737) directly challenges conventional 
SEO wisdom and has significant implications for B2B SaaS content strategy.

## Post 2 — AI Content Helper Launch

Very excited about this: Ahrefs AI Content Helper is now live (in early beta).

Every serious content team uses some type of content optimization tool. But 
many of these tools have a dark side. They reward keyword-stuffing, ruining 
serviceable article drafts with post-hoc keyword cramming.

AI Content Helper is different: Instead of counting terms that you need to 
include in your content, Content Helper uses AI to identify the core topics 
for your target keywords, and scores your content (as well as your 
competitors) against those topics as you write it.

The goal isn't keyword stuffing: it's comprehensive topic coverage.

**Annotation:** Ryan's product thinking here — AI as a research and coverage 
tool, not a writing replacement — represents the dominant practitioner 
philosophy in 2025-26. This framing is directly applicable to content 
production workflows at B2B SaaS companies like 100Hires.
