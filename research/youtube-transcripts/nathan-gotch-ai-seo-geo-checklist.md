## Core Framework Covered

**The Three Pillars of Modern Search Visibility**

Nathan structures the entire checklist around three interconnected 
layers that every brand needs to optimize simultaneously:

**Layer 1 — SEO (Search Engine Optimization)**
- Foundation layer — cannot be skipped or deprioritized
- Crawlability, indexation, technical health
- Classic on-page signals still matter
- Without strong SEO, GEO efforts are undermined

**Layer 2 — AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)**
- Structuring content for featured snippets and zero-click answers
- Voice assistant optimization
- FAQ and structured data implementation
- Focuses on how questions get answered directly in search results

**Layer 3 — GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)**
- Optimizing so AI-powered tools cite your content
- Target platforms: ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, AI Overviews
- Goal: become the source these systems pull from when answering 
  queries in your category

## Key Checklist Items (from video summary)

**Content Structure for AI**
- Write content that directly answers specific questions
- Use clear headers that mirror how people ask questions to AI
- Include data, statistics, and original research — AI systems 
  prefer citable, verifiable content
- Avoid vague claims; be specific and attributable

**Technical Requirements**
- Ensure pages are crawlable by AI bots (not just Googlebot)
- JavaScript rendering issues block AI crawlers — server-side 
  rendering preferred
- Structured data (FAQ schema, HowTo schema) helps AI systems 
  understand content structure
- Page speed and Core Web Vitals still relevant for RAG retrieval

**Authority and Trust Signals**
- Third-party mentions across authoritative sites
- Consistent brand presence across multiple platforms
- Author expertise signals — named authors with verifiable 
  credentials perform better in AI citations
- Community presence (Reddit, Quora, industry forums) — 
  AI systems train on these platforms

**The Spam Strategy to Avoid**
- Publishing hundreds of AI-generated articles with no editorial 
  oversight
- Self-promotional listicles at scale
- Content that exists purely to game AI citation — gets detected 
  and demoted

## Nathan's Core Thesis

"AI = terrible at SEO. AI led by experienced SEO pro = domination."

The checklist is built on this philosophy: AI tools handle the 
mechanical, repeatable parts of content production. Experienced 
practitioners provide the strategic judgment, quality control, 
and expertise that AI cannot replicate.

---

## Why This Video Matters for B2B SaaS

The 97-point checklist is directly applicable to a B2B SaaS 
content team:

- Most B2B SaaS companies have strong Layer 1 (SEO) but weak 
  Layer 2 (AEO) and almost no Layer 3 (GEO)
- The FAQ schema and structured data recommendations are 
  low-effort, high-impact quick wins
- The community presence recommendations (Reddit, Quora) are 
  particularly relevant for ATS/HR software — these communities 
  are heavily cited by AI when answering "what's the best 
  recruiting software?" type queries
